the "train without tracks"
we want to design a train that can travel through the forest, without the need for tracks.
the train will operate similar to a tank; it'll have treads that run along a track 
coming down from each segment (of which, there will be hundreds) will be a series of loosenable / tightenable "legs"
when they are tight, they become rigid.. at the front of our train, these "leg-chains" can be seen descending, and being almost "floppy" a tiny bit
however; as they've reached to being "under" the main tank - each of the dozens of legs is now rigid
the train will be able to "walk" over the terrain, and will be able to climb up and down hills, and even over small mountains

the "legs" - each leg will be a series of 100 or so "segments" .. segments are non-descript.
they'll go straight down, except when moving up/down at back/front
the segments will appear to be somewhat "spindly".. their strenth is in numbers. each has a girth of only 3-5 inches 
there wont be any "joints" .. these legs serve only as a stilt, for the trackless-train, going by overhead
these trains are designed to pass seemless by trees.. we want this depicted with trees directly under the train (your previous version had a clearing.. which is the opposite of the "unique" feature of this train)

there will be no "train tracks" on the ground - that's another goal; we want "trains without tracks" , as tracks are destructive to the environment 


we want to add one of these, appearing to be about a mile in length, and also a mile away from the camera
